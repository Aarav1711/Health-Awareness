Why It Matters

Academic stress peaks during exams, deadlines, and transitions. Unmanaged stress can spiral into anxiety disorders, depression, and burnout — all preventable with the right tools.

The Silent Epidemic

Most students suffer in silence. Campus counselling is underutilised. By raising awareness, we empower students to recognise signs and take action early — before it becomes a crisis.

📋 Overview

What Are Stress & Anxiety?

Understanding the difference is the first step to managing them.

😰

Stress

A normal physiological response to external demands — exams, deadlines, social pressures. Stress fades once the trigger is resolved.

  • Short-term & situational
  • Linked to a clear cause
  • Can motivate action
  • Disappears after resolution
😟

Anxiety

Persistent, excessive worry or fear that remains even without a clear trigger. Anxiety can interfere with daily life if untreated.

  • Ongoing and persistent
  • Often without a clear cause
  • Causes avoidance behaviour
  • May need professional support
⚖️

Why It Matters

Both affect academic performance, physical health, and social wellbeing. Recognising which you face shapes how you respond.

  • Affects 75% of students
  • Can lead to depression
  • Treatable & manageable
  • Early awareness = better outcomes
💡

Did you know?

The World Health Organisation identifies depression and anxiety as leading causes of disability worldwide. Students aged 18–24 are among the highest-risk groups. Recognising early signs can prevent long-term mental health conditions.

The Student Experience

From the moment students enter college or university, they face a rapid increase in responsibilities — academic workload, social navigation, financial pressures, and future planning all simultaneously.

This creates a perfect storm for chronic stress. The brain's threat-detection system (amygdala) stays activated, flooding the body with cortisol and adrenaline — affecting memory, decision-making, immune response, and sleep.

📉 GPA impact 💤 Sleep disruption 🤝 Social withdrawal 🧪 Hormonal changes

When to Seek Help Immediately

If symptoms persist for more than 2 weeks, interfere with daily functioning, or include thoughts of self-harm — please reach out to a mental health professional or crisis line right away.

🛡️ Coping

Coping Strategies & Tools

Evidence-based techniques to manage stress and anxiety in daily student life.

01
🧘

Mindfulness

Even 5–10 min daily can rewire your brain's stress response. Try body scans, guided meditation, or mindful eating.

Apps: Headspace, Calm
02
🏃

Exercise

Releases endorphins. A 20-minute walk reduces cortisol significantly and boosts mood immediately.

Goal: 30 min/day
03
📓

Journaling

Writing externalises worries. Gratitude journaling trains the brain to notice the positive.

Write 3 things daily
04
⏰

Time Management

Use Pomodoro technique — 25 min focus + 5 min break. Reduces overwhelm through small steps.

Pomodoro technique
05
😴

Sleep Hygiene

Poor sleep amplifies stress. Keep consistent schedule, avoid screens 1 hour before bed.

Aim: 7–9 hours/night
06
🤝

Social Support

Talk to a trusted friend, family, or counsellor. Sharing burdens reduces their weight.

Join support groups

✅ Healthy Coping

  • Talk about your feelings
  • Take regular breaks
  • Practice deep breathing
  • Maintain a daily routine
  • Eat balanced meals
  • Seek professional help

❌ Avoid These

  • Isolating yourself
  • Excessive alcohol / substances
  • Binge-watching to escape
  • Skipping meals or overeating
  • Ignoring the problem
  • Lashing out at others
